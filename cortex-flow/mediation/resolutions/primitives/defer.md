# Primitive: defer

## Meaning

The tension is acknowledged, but mediation is postponed intentionally. Deferral is a deliberate choice to not resolve now, with explicit conditions under which the deferral ends.

## What it is

- an explicit, traceable decision to postpone mediation
- a contextual acknowledgment that the current moment is not appropriate for resolution
- a placeholder that preserves the tension in view

## What it is not

- ignoring the tension
- losing track of the concern
- silent acceptance
- an equivalent of `accept-temporarily` (deferral postpones mediation; temporary acceptance takes a bounded position)

## Required fields

- originating tension(s)
- reason for deferral
- conditions under which mediation will be resumed (event, date, contextual change)
- owner of the deferral (who is responsible for resuming mediation)

## Drift risk

Without an explicit resumption condition, `defer` becomes indistinguishable from organizational forgetting. A deferral without a resumption condition is not well-formed.
