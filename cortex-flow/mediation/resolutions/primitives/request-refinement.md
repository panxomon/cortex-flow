# Primitive: request-refinement

## Meaning

The tension cannot be resolved under the current context because contextual ambiguity is too high. The response is **reduction of ambiguity**, not correction of the author.

## What it is

- a request to reduce contextual ambiguity
- a signal that interpretation cannot stabilize with the information available
- an invitation to clarify context, constraints, intent, or scope

## What it is not

- a correction directed at the author of the artifact
- a rejection
- a quality judgment
- a governance directive

## Required fields

- originating tension(s)
- what is ambiguous (context, constraints, intent, scope, other)
- what kind of refinement would reduce the ambiguity
- whether the refinement is expected from the operator, the author, or the context itself

## Drift risk

Without care, `request-refinement` can be read as `your output is wrong, fix it`. The vocabulary must stay anchored in ambiguity reduction, not authorship correction. If the refinement request implicitly blames the author, it is malformed.
