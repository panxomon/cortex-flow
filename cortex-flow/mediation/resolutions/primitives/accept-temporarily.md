# Primitive: accept-temporarily

## Meaning

The artifact is acceptable **within a bounded contextual window**. Stabilization is pending further contextual evolution, not achieved.

## What it is

- stabilization pending further contextual evolution
- a bounded position with an explicit revisit condition
- a traceable acknowledgment of tension that remains unresolved in principle but tolerable in context

## What it is not

- convergence of meaning
- permanent stability
- an automatic generator of strong precedent
- a quiet equivalent of `accept`

## Required fields

- originating tension(s)
- contextual rationale
- active policy reference
- explicit temporal bound (event, release, date, or contextual change)
- explicit revisit condition
- what specifically remains unresolved

## Drift risk

`accept-temporarily` is the primitive most prone to decay into `accept and forget`. Without an explicit temporal bound AND an explicit revisit condition, this resolution is not well-formed. The runtime must refuse to record it without both fields present.
