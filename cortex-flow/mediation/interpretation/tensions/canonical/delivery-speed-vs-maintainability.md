# Tension: delivery-speed-vs-maintainability

> The conflict between producing working outcomes quickly and producing outcomes that remain comprehensible, modifiable, and safe to change over time.

---

## Competing concerns

- delivering value to users or stakeholders as fast as possible
- keeping the system understandable and changeable for future work

---

## Common contexts where it emerges

- early-stage products under market pressure
- time-boxed deliveries and deadlines
- prototypes that may evolve into production systems
- feature work in systems with existing debt
- migrations under business constraints

---

## Typical tradeoffs

- favoring delivery speed: faster time-to-value, earlier feedback, higher future modification cost, increased cognitive load for maintainers
- favoring maintainability: slower initial delivery, lower long-term cost, higher resilience to change, risk of over-engineering when context does not require it
